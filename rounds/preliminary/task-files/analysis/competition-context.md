## Core Topic

The case asks teams to help L'Oreal China answer one central question:

> In the Chinese market, how should L'Oreal formulate an AI-driven brand marketing strategy that builds authentic, deep interaction with Gen Z consumers while achieving sustainable brand growth?

The answer must focus on one specific brand under the L'Oreal Group and analyze that brand's particular strategic situation in China.

## Background Logic

The beauty industry is shifting from "selling products" to "building relationships."

The old growth model was relatively clear:

- celebrity endorsement
- channel distribution
- promotion-driven sales
- brand-led definition of beauty

This model is becoming less effective because Gen Z consumers are no longer passive recipients of brand narratives. They increasingly:

- build their own beauty identities
- rely on real user content and community word of mouth
- move across fragmented, nonlinear purchase journeys
- expect dialogue with brands instead of one-way selling
- expect brands to understand them, not only deliver product efficacy

At the same time, category boundaries are blurring:

- skincare and health
- content and retail
- online and offline
- public traffic and private-domain operations

For L'Oreal, the strategic challenge is how a global beauty group with many brands and massive consumer reach can still create deep resonance with each individual consumer.

## AI Context

AI is becoming a new competitive divider in beauty marketing. However, the brief warns that most current AI use cases remain at the efficiency layer:

- more precise ad targeting
- more automated customer service
- faster content generation

These are useful, but they do not fully answer the deeper strategic question:

> Can AI reshape the relationship between brands and consumers?

L'Oreal's broader vision in the brief is:

> Beauty for Each, Powered by Beauty Tech

The expected direction is not generic AI adoption, but translating this vision into an actionable brand strategy that creates intelligent, empathetic, and warm relationships with next-generation consumers.

## Required Analysis

The official brief asks teams to cover three major areas.

### 1. Trends And Challenges

Analyze external and internal trends or challenges, including:

- China's beauty industry shift from product selling to relationship building
- the current layout and limitations of AI applications
- the selected brand's most important external opportunities
- the selected brand's key internal challenges in AI marketing transformation

### 2. Strategic Positioning And AI Initiatives

Define the selected brand's strategic positioning in Gen Z consumer interaction.

From a business and consumer-journey perspective, propose 1-2 concrete and executable AI-enabled scenarios, applications, or marketing campaigns.

The proposal must explain:

- how the initiatives reshape consumer relationships
- how they drive measurable brand growth
- why they are commercially grounded

The brief explicitly asks teams to focus on commercial scenarios, not underlying technical development or isolated feature design.

### 3. Rollout Path And Risk Assessment

Identify key non-technical barriers, such as:

- organization
- data
- channels
- execution coordination
- trust and governance

Then design:

- a phased implementation roadmap
- high-level risk response suggestions
- a practical path to execution

## Important Warning From The Brief

The brief explicitly warns teams to avoid product thinking.

The case is not asking teams to design a single function. It is testing whether teams can:

- make strategic choices
- explain why those choices are right
- show how to promote implementation
- connect initiatives to commercial logic

Therefore, later work should avoid generic answers such as:

- AI skin diagnosis as a standalone function
- virtual try-on as a standalone function
- AI customer service as a standalone function
- AI-generated content as an efficiency tool only

Any AI idea should be framed as part of brand strategy and consumer relationship reconstruction.

## Initial Working Implications

For future work, the preliminary round should be approached as a brand-specific strategy case, not a generic L'Oreal group-level AI proposal.

The strongest answer will likely need to:

- select one brand with a clear relationship problem
- identify a sharp consumer and market contradiction
- explain how AI changes the brand-consumer relationship
- propose only 1-2 high-quality initiatives
- connect the initiatives to measurable growth
- show phased execution and non-technical risk awareness

Useful working thesis:

> AI should be treated not as a marketing efficiency tool, but as brand relationship infrastructure: a system that helps the brand understand consumers dynamically, co-create meaning with them, and turn repeated interactions into long-term growth assets.
