## Slide 2: Industry Change And Relationship Reconstruction

### Evidence 1: Luxury consumers expect more than transaction

Source: Vogue Business / Bain luxury forecast  
URL: https://www.vogue.com/article/luxury-will-make-a-slow-recovery-in-2026-bain-forecasts

Usable evidence:

- 2026 global luxury spending is forecast to grow slowly after a two-year downturn.
- In China, online luxury sales grew strongly in Q1 2026 versus the prior year, with Bain attributing some category shift to self-expression rather than status signaling.
- Luxury experiences are outperforming personal goods in consumer sentiment.
- Brands need more personalized journeys and stores as experience spaces, not just transaction points.
- Around half of luxury consumers have used AI in shopping journeys; use cases include brand discovery and product comparison.
- Gen Z loyalty is relatively low; Gen Z evaluates brands carefully and seeks meaning and relevance.
- Bain/Vogue Business highlight culture, events, sports, and authentic connection as important for future luxury growth.

Slide implication:

> High-end beauty cannot rely only on luxury symbols. The category needs relationship mechanisms that create meaning, relevance, personalization, and participatory experiences.

### Evidence 2: Beauty market is saturated and consumers are price-sensitive

Source: Vogue Business Beauty Index 2025  
URL: https://www.vogue.com/article/tariffs-price-hikes-and-manda-2025s-beauty-landscape-rewrites-the-vogue-business-beauty-index

Usable evidence:

- 31% of beauty shoppers say they plan to buy from less expensive brands if prices rise.
- Beauty brands compete for attention in a saturated field.
- Physical pop-ups can drive UGC and consumer engagement.
- Fewer brands are investing in gamified experiences and NFTs; attention is shifting toward tangible innovation and AI in R&D/product development.
- The index evaluates beauty brands across consumer sentiment, digital marketing, ESG, and innovation.

Slide implication:

> Beauty growth is under pressure from price sensitivity and attention saturation. Brands need interaction formats that justify value and produce consumer participation, not just exposure.

### Evidence 3: AI in beauty is often service, diagnostic, recommendation, or efficiency oriented

Source: Vogue Business Meaningful AI Luxury Summit  
URL: https://www.vogue.com/article/finding-meaning-in-the-age-of-ai

Usable evidence:

- L'Oreal frames beauty tech as augmenting humans and removing barriers for shoppers.
- Examples include Beauty Genius, skincare diagnostics, recommendations, filters, shade finders, and assistive beauty devices.
- Luxury AI discussion also warns that AI should improve work without replacing creativity and craftsmanship.

Slide implication:

> AI has clear value in access, personalization, and service, but the case opportunity is to go beyond efficiency and tools into brand relationship reconstruction.

## Slide 3: YSL Opportunities And Challenges

### Evidence 4: YSL Beauty is already experimenting with Gen Z cultural participation

Source: Vogue Business - Inside YSL Beauty's Gen Z Playbook  
URL: https://www.vogue.com/article/inside-ysl-beautys-gen-z-playbook

Usable evidence:

- YSL Beauty launched block-party-style activations in local cultural venues.
- The strategy emphasizes cultural credibility, emotional connection, local nuances, and participation rather than traditional step-and-repeat luxury events.
- Examples include local places such as a cafe, pub, laundromat, or Shanghai karaoke bar.
- YSL tracks not only impressions and reach, but the quality of conversation around events.
- Guests can bring friends, making the experience less closed and more socially authentic.
- Gen Z values personalized real-life experiences comparable to online personalization.
- YSL's makeup artist framing emphasizes makeup as dialogue with who the user is that day, not perfection.

Slide implication:

> YSL's strongest opportunity is not generic AI beauty utility, but turning self-expression and local culture into a scalable online-offline relationship loop.

### Evidence 5: YSL brand DNA supports bold self-expression

Source: L'Oreal Group official YSL Beauty page  
URL: https://www.loreal.com/en/luxe-division/yves-saint-laurent/

Usable evidence:

- YSL Beauty is positioned inside L'Oreal Luxe.
- Official brand language emphasizes boldness, singularity, freedom from convention, and distinctive luxury.

Slide implication:

> The AI strategy must preserve YSL's luxury and attitude codes. It cannot feel like a generic beauty recommendation feature.
