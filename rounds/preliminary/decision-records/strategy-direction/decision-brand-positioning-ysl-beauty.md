# Decision: Select YSL Beauty As The Preliminary-Round Brand

Date: 2026-06-27

## Context

The team compared highlighted L'Oreal brands for the preliminary-round case. The case requires one specific brand and asks for an AI-driven marketing strategy that reconstructs the brand-consumer relationship with China Gen Z consumers while driving measurable growth.

The shortlisted brands included:

- 3CE Stylenanda
- YSL Beauty
- Kiehl's
- L'Oreal Professionnel
- Maybelline New York
- L'Oreal Paris

## Decision

Formally select **YSL Beauty** as the brand for the preliminary-round strategy.

The working strategic positioning is:

> YSL Beauty as China's Gen Z high-end self-expression co-creator.

## Rationale

YSL Beauty is a strong fit because its brand DNA already points to boldness, self-expression, rule-breaking, young luxury, and cultural attitude. These assets make it more suitable for an AI relationship strategy than a generic AI beauty tool.

The team believes YSL's strategic opportunity is not to use AI for ordinary try-on, color recommendation, customer service, or content automation. Instead, AI should help translate YSL's abstract brand spirit into personalized, participatory, and commercially measurable consumer relationships.

The current strategic thesis is:

> YSL Beauty can move from a high-end beauty symbol to a relationship system that helps China Gen Z define, perform, share, and retain their own high-end self-expression.

## Key Implications

- The deck should focus on YSL Beauty rather than a broader L'Oreal group-level solution.
- AI should be framed as an **Attitude Curator**, not as a standalone functional tool.
- The main AI scenarios should connect brand attitude, consumer identity, social content, offline experience, membership, and product conversion.
- Research should now prioritize YSL-specific China evidence, including Xiaohongshu, Douyin, Tmall/JD, WeChat/private-domain touchpoints, and existing brand activations.
- Visual direction should preserve YSL's premium, sharp, bold, high-contrast tone and avoid cheap or overly playful AI aesthetics.

## Open Questions

- How do China Gen Z consumers currently describe YSL Beauty on social and commerce platforms?
- Which YSL categories and occasions should anchor the strategy: lipstick, fragrance, cushion, gifting, events, stores, or membership?
- How can AI personalization preserve YSL's luxury curation authority rather than over-adapting to every user preference?
- Which metrics best prove relationship reconstruction: member registration, saved profiles, UGC quality, store visits, bundle conversion, repurchase, or LTV?
