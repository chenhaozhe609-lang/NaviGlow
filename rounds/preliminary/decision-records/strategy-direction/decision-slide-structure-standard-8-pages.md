# Decision: Adopt Standard 8-Page Slide Structure

Date: 2026-06-27

## Context

The team discussed the preliminary-round deck structure after the first batch of meeting notes. The official requirement asks for a strategy case, not a feature proposal, and the deck must clearly cover:

- trends, external opportunities, and internal challenges
- strategic positioning and AI initiatives
- rollout path and risk assessment

The team proposed the following high-level structure:

1. Strategic summary
2. Industry analysis plus brand external opportunities and internal challenges
3. Strategic positioning
4. Two application scenarios
5. Rollout path and risk assessment

## Options Considered

### Option A: Standard 8-page body

- Slide 1: Strategic summary
- Slide 2: Industry change and consumer relationship reconstruction
- Slide 3: YSL external opportunities and internal challenges
- Slide 4: Strategic positioning
- Slide 5: YSL Attitude Code
- Slide 6: YSL City Afterglow
- Slide 7: Rollout path
- Slide 8: Risk assessment and response

### Option B: Compressed 7-page body

Merge industry analysis and YSL opportunity/challenge diagnosis into one page.

## Decision

Adopt **Option A: Standard 8-page body** as the formal slide information architecture.

## Rationale

The second section is strategically important and should not be over-compressed. Splitting it into two pages gives the deck enough space to prove:

- why AI-driven relationship reconstruction matters now
- why YSL Beauty has a distinctive external opportunity
- why YSL faces brand-specific internal challenges in AI marketing transformation

This structure also maps cleanly to the official case requirements and avoids turning the diagnosis page into a dense report screenshot.

## Implications

- Future slide writing, evidence collection, visual design, and speaking notes should follow the 8-page body structure.
- The industry diagnosis and YSL opportunity/challenge analysis should be developed as separate slides.
- The compressed 7-page structure is no longer the active working version.
- Any later change to page count or section order should be recorded as a new decision.
